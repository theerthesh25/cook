VEG RAMEN NOODLES

A simple recipe for making Vegetarian Ramen Noodles

Ingredients


For the Broth

8 cups vegetable broth
2 cloves garlic,minced
1-inch piece of ginger,grated
2 tablespoons soy sauce
1 tablespoon miso paste (optional)
1 teaspoon sesame oil (optional)
Salt and pepper to taste

For the Ramen

8 ounces ramen noodles
2 cups sliced vegetables (e.g, bok choy, carrots, mushrooms, bell peppers, and green onions)
1 cup tofu, cubed (optional)
1 boiled egg, halved (optional)
Sesame seeds and nori (seaweed) for garnish (optional)

Steps


Prepare the Broth

In a large pot, heat vegetable broth over medium heat.
Add minced garlic, grated ginger, soy sauce, miso paste (if using), and sesame oil (if using).
Stir to combine.
Let the broth simmer for about 15-20 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Prepare the Ramen Noodles

While the broth is simmering, cook the ramen noodles according to the package instructions.
Usually, you'll need to boil them for 2-3 minutes.
Drain the noodles and rinse them with cold water to stop the cooking process.
This helps prevent them from getting too soft and sticky.
Set aside.

Prepare the Vegetables

While the noodles are cooking, prepare your choice of vegetables.
You can saut√© them lightly in a separate pan with a bit of oil for added flavor or add them directly to the simmering broth to cook until tender.

Prepare Tofu (if using)

If you're using tofu, you can pan-fry or bake it until it's lightly golden on the outside.
Season with a bit of soy sauce or salt for extra flavor.

Assemble the Ramen

Divide the cooked ramen noodles into serving bowls.
Ladle the hot broth over the noodles.
Make sure to include the vegetables and tofu.
If desired, add a halved boiled egg on top.

Garnish and Serve

Garnish your veggie ramen with sesame seeds and nori (seaweed) for an authentic touch.Enjoy!
Serve your homemade Vegetarian Ramen Noodles hot and enjoy a comforting and flavorful meal.
Feel free to customize your ramen by adding other ingredients like corn, bean sprouts, or bamboo shoots to suit your taste.
